What You’re Stepping Into

A supportive space to grow.

Make Art Make Money is a 4-week workshop to help you clarify your values, build a supportive network, and grow a creative business that feels true to your work.

1

A small, intentional group

Limited to 10 participants, so the space can stay focused, supportive, and genuinely collaborative. We establish shared community agreements and clear expectations around care, respect, and participation.

2

An exploratory + practical curriculum

Each session blends creative prompts, reflection, and storytelling with concrete tools—helping you move from ideas into structure, language, and real-world application.

3

Committed mentorship + real-world perspective

This course is designed and taught by working artists and creative professionals who are deeply invested in building New Mexico’s creative business ecosystem—and in helping you succeed.

You’ll also connect with creative entrepreneurs, mentors, and the broader ecosystem through Creative Con and beyond.

4

Support from a larger creative ecosystem

This workshop is an entry point into a broader effort to support underrepresented and emerging creative businesses in New Mexico—connecting you to the tools, networks, and opportunities needed to grow sustainably.

You won’t get lost in this. You’ll be supported, challenged, and part of something people are actively building—together.
Why This Exists

Artists deserve better business support.

Most business programs weren’t built for artists. You’re expected to figure out pricing, messaging, funding, and sustainability—often alone, and often in ways that don’t feel aligned with your work.

Make Art Make Money is Different. We start with your creative practice.

Together, we identify your gifts, strengths, and the values that drive your work. From there, we build outward—helping you clearly articulate your vision and the impact of what you make, so you can translate your practice into a business that feels genuinely aligned.

So you can move forward with clarity—and the confidence to build a creative business that feels true to your vision.
Who This Is For

For artists, creative entrepreneurs building something real.

We’re bringing together artists and creative entrepreneurs who want to take their vision to the next level, build something real—and want support doing it.

What You’ll Walk Away With

Clarity, language, and a structure you can actually use.

Through creative prompts, zines, and hands-on exercises, we’ll help you unlock:

1

Clarity on your work and direction

A deeper understanding of your strengths, your context, and the role your work plays in your community.

2

Language to talk about what you do

The ability to clearly communicate your work—whether you’re speaking to collaborators, funders, or audiences.

3

A way to think about money that actually fits your practice

Tools for understanding revenue, costs, audiences, and business models—without forcing your work into something it’s not.

Who’s Holding This Space

Working artists building real infrastructure for creatives.

Mayowa Tomori

Artist, producer, and co-founder of AMPL Labs, a multidisciplinary creative studio focused on building tools, systems, and support structures for creative entrepreneurs.

His work spans storytelling, technology, and community-led design, with a focus on helping artists and founders translate their ideas into sustainable, real-world ventures.

He has received over $250,000 in grants and fellowships from organizations including Mozilla, the Smithsonian, and the National Institutes of Health.

Lauren Pellerano Gomez (Guest Lecturer)

Marketing and communications expert with over 10 years of experience supporting startups and creative businesses.

Through LPG Consulting (Santa Fe), they help early-stage ventures find product-market fit, build brand positioning, and scale sustainably.

Their work spans clients like Google, Reebok, and Converse, along with deep experience supporting BIPOC and immigrant entrepreneurs.

Lauren and Mayowa are actively building a creative incubator designed to support artists and underrepresented founders in New Mexico with the tools, infrastructure, and community they need to grow sustainable businesses.
